Baked French Toast with Caramelized Pears and Hazelnuts

Baked French Toast with Pears and Hazelnuts– a delicious fall-inspired brunch recipe.

In France, French toast is called pain perdu, which literally means “lost bread”. The unused, leftover and stale bits of bread are soaked in a batter of cream and eggs, and then fried, transforming the bread into a delicious hearty meal.

Nothing is wasted. This French toast recipe is baked instead of fried and can be assembled the day before, which makes it easy if you are having guests over. It’s a cozy and decadent recipe featuring fall pears caramelized in butter and maple syrup with toasted hazelnuts and real vanilla beans. It’s perfect for the holidays or a special occasion brunch.


Ingredients ⇩

  • 7–8 cups French bread (about one large loaf) or baguette or brioche bread
  • 2 ripe Bosc pears
  • 1/2 cup toasted hazelnuts, crushed
  • 7 eggs
  • 2 1/2 cups half-and-half (or use whole milk)
  • 1/2 cup real maple syrup (divided)
  • 1/8 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom
  • 1 vanilla bean (or 1 tsp vanilla extract)
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Zest of one small orange

Instructions ⇩

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. Toast hazelnuts for 10-15 minutes, let cool, and gently crush using a rolling pin.
  3. Split the vanilla bean, scrape out seeds, and place directly in a small saucepan with 2 1/2 cups of half-and-half. Add the remaining vanilla pod. Warm up the half-and-half and vanilla on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until almost to a boil. Turn the heat off and let it cool and seep for 20-30 minutes.
  4. Slice the pears. Melt the butter in a skillet and sauté pears on medium heat until golden, about 8-10 minutes. Add 1 tablespoon of maple syrup. Simmer for a few more minutes, allowing them to caramelize. Set aside.
  5. Cut the bread into 1/2 inch slices. In a medium bowl, beat eggs, salt, spices, orange zest, and 3 tablespoons of maple syrup. When the half-and-half has cooled, remove the vanilla pod, and add a little at a time into the egg mixture, mixing it together. (Do NOT add hot cream to the egg mixture, as it may cause your eggs to curdle. Either let the cream cool or temper the eggs by adding just a little warm cream at a time, mixing in between so the eggs gradually warm up.)
  6. Generously butter a 9x13-inch baking dish. Layer the bread, hazelnuts, and caramelized pears, pouring all the buttery syrup from the pan evenly over the bread. Pour the batter over, using a spatula to ensure it goes into all the cracks and crevices. Let sit for at least 30 minutes so the bread can soak up the batter, or refrigerate overnight.
  7. Right before placing in the oven, drizzle the top with 1 more tablespoon of maple syrup and sprinkle with a pinch of cardamom.
  8. Bake for 45-50 minutes until puffed and golden. If your layers measure higher than two inches, you may need to bake longer. Check the middle to see if all the liquid is cooked. The true test is if it puffs up; it is done.
  9. Melt small dots of butter over the top and serve with warm maple syrup.
