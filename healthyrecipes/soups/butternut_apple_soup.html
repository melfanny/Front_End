Butternut and Apple Soup

Cozy up with a bowl of creamy Roasted Butternut Squash Soup with crispy sage, coconut milk, and pumpkin seed brittle. Vegan and delicious.

This Roasted Butternut Squash Soup recipe is so cozy and comforting! It’s made with very simple ingredients, yet feels decadent! Vegan and gluten-free, it is topped with a swirl of warmed coconut milk, crispy sage leaves and toasted pumpkin seed brittle.

Along with onion, garlic, and ginger, a little apple gives this butternut squash soup recipe a hint of sweetness and tartness to balance the creamy, nutty, earthy flavor of the butternut squash. Butternuts and Apples love to be together and complement each other well. A good marriage.


Ingredients ⇩

  • 2 tablespoons coconut oil, olive oil, or butter
  • 1 large apple (Gala, Honey Crisp), diced (more for garnish, optional)
  • 2 large shallots (or 1 onion), diced
  • 2–4 garlic cloves, rough chopped
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, rough chopped (or ginger paste)
  • 1 tablespoon fresh sage, more for garnish
  • 3 1/2–4 cups vegetable stock or chicken stock (or 4 cups water with 2 teaspoons veggie bouillon paste)
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon fresh cracked pepper
  • Fresh grated nutmeg (optional, about 1/4 teaspoon, more to taste)
  • Pinch cayenne (optional)
  • 1/2–3/4 cup coconut milk (or 1/4-1/2 cup heavy cream or half and half, or sub almond milk for a lighter version)
  • 1–2 teaspoons maple syrup (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon apple cider vinegar (optional)
  • Optional garnishes: crispy sage, Pumpkin seed “Brittle”, sumac, warm coconut milk, sautéed diced apple

Instructions ⇩

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
  2. Split the butternut squash in half lengthwise, place it open-side down on a greased or parchment-lined, rimmed sheet pan. Roast for 30-35 minutes until easily pierced with a fork.
  3. While the butternut is roasting, chop the onion, apple, garlic, ginger, and herbs.
  4. Heat oil in a large heavy-bottom pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  5. Add onion and apple and cook for 6-7 minutes, stirring until golden and tender. Then, add garlic and ginger, and cook for 4-5 more minutes. Add the herbs and cook for one more minute. Turn heat off.
  6. When the butternut is tender, flip it over and let it cool enough to handle. Scoop out the seeds and flesh, yielding 3 3/4 to 4 cups.
  7. Add the apple-onion mixture to the butternut squash.
  8. Blend the mixture in batches with vegetable stock until very smooth using a blender. (Be careful with hot ingredients—hold the blender lid down with a kitchen towel.)
  9. Pour the blended soup back into the pot and warm over low heat. Add salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Stir in your choice of milk to the desired richness.
  10. Taste the soup. If you prefer more sweetness, add maple syrup. If you need acidity, add apple cider vinegar or a squeeze of orange juice. Adjust salt to taste.
  11. Serve in bowls and garnish with maple-glazed pumpkin seeds, crispy sage leaves, and sautéed apple, if desired.
