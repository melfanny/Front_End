Strawberry Galette

This Strawberry Galette is bright, cheery, and full of springtime flavor. A rustic flaky pastry crust encloses a fresh strawberry filling -lemon and rosemary perfectly complement the sweet-tart berries.

This Strawberry Galette is the happiest little treat, perfect for brunch or dessert. It is best made with garden fresh strawberries, if possible. Rosemary and lemon zest give the perfect complementing flavors to the sweet-tart berries.


Ingredients ⇩

Crust

  • 1 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (156 grams)
  • 1/4 cup rye flour (25 grams) or substitute more all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 10 tablespoons butter, chilled
  • 1/4 – 1/3 cup ice water
  • Optional: 1 egg for egg wash and coarse sugar for sprinkling

Filling

  • 1 1/2 lb strawberries (about 3 pints), sliced in half or quarters lengthwise
  • 1 teaspoon lemon zest
  • 1 teaspoon rosemary
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions ⇩

  1. Toast the rye flour in a skillet over medium-low heat, stirring until golden (about 5 minutes). Let cool.
  2. In a food processor, add both flours, salt, and sugar, and pulse to mix. Add the chilled butter and pulse until the mixture is granular and resembles coarse sand.
  3. With the food processor running, gradually add the cold water until the dough just comes together into a shaggy ball. Do not over-mix.
  4. On a floured piece of parchment, shape the dough into a log about 2 inches in diameter. Wrap it in parchment and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or up to 3 days.
  5. Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place a baking sheet in the oven to preheat.
  6. Roll out the refrigerated dough into a roughly 14-inch circle. Transfer it to a sheet of parchment paper and set aside while you prepare the filling.
  7. In a large bowl, combine the strawberries, lemon zest, and rosemary. In a small bowl, mix together the sugar, salt, and cornstarch. Stir the sugar mixture into the strawberries quickly to avoid clumping.
  8. Scoop the strawberry mixture onto the center of the dough. Arrange the strawberries cut side down for a more attractive appearance.
  9. Fold 2 inches of dough over the filling, crimping every 2 inches to create a rustic edge.
  10. Brush the crust with egg wash (1 egg whisked with 1 tablespoon of water, cream, or milk), and sprinkle with coarse sugar if desired.
  11. Remove the heated baking sheet from the oven, and transfer the galette (with parchment) onto the sheet. Place it in the lower part of the oven.
  12. Bake for 20 minutes until the crust is golden and the filling is bubbling in the center.
  13. Let the galette rest for at least 1 hour before serving.
