Pavlova

Making a Pavlova is fun and easy. Top with fresh berries and lofty whipped cream. A light and airy dessert perfect for celebrations and gatherings.

Pavlova is a light and airy dessert, hailing from Australia and New Zealand made of whipped egg whites, that are slow-baked in the oven until crisp. Typically topped with whipped cream and fruit, it’s a stunning dessert, that looks fancy, but couldn’t be easier!

This Pavlova recipe is topped with various fresh seasonal berries and whipped cream. So delicious! Keep it simple or enhance your whipped cream with a dollop of jam or lemon curd. There is something so satisfying about whipping egg whites into fluffy white clouds. If you’ve never tried it, we promise you will love it.


Ingredients ⇩

Pavlova

  • 4 large eggs (at room temperature)
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup fine sugar (see notes)
  • 1 teaspoon cream of tartar (or 1 teaspoon vinegar – see notes)
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract (optional)

Whipped Cream

  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (adjust to your preferred sweetness)
  • Lemon zest or Meyer lemon zest (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon berry jam (or lemon curd)

Berry Topping

  • 1 pint fresh berries (blackberries, blueberries, strawberries, currants, or a mix)

Instructions ⇩

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C). Draw an 8-9″ circle on a piece of parchment paper and place it on a baking sheet. Flip the parchment so the writing is on the underside.
  2. Separate the eggs. Cold eggs separate better, but room-temperature egg whites whip better. Separate the eggs while cold, then let the whites come to room temperature. To speed this up, place the bowl of whites over a bowl of warm water.
  3. In a stand mixer, whip the egg whites on medium until foamy. Add cream of tartar. If using vinegar instead, add it later with the vanilla.
  4. Gradually add sugar one tablespoon at a time, whisking between each addition until the sugar is fully incorporated. Increase the mixer speed and beat until the whites are glossy and stiff.
  5. Test the mixture by rubbing between your fingers; there should be no sugar granules. If it's still gritty, continue beating.
  6. Once the peaks are stiff, add the pinch of salt and vanilla (if using) and whip for another 30 seconds.
  7. Shape the Pavlova: Gently scoop the mixture onto the parchment. Spread it in a circle with a cake knife, creating a slight indent in the center with raised edges.
  8. Bake: Place the tray in the oven, then reduce the temperature to 200°F (95°C). Bake for 120 minutes or until the Pavlova is dry to the touch. Turn off the oven and allow it to cool inside for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  9. For the whipped cream: Whip together the cream, sugar, and zest to medium-soft peaks. Fold in a tablespoon of jam or lemon curd if desired.
  10. Assemble: Just before serving, top the cooled Pavlova with whipped cream and fresh berries. Serve immediately!
