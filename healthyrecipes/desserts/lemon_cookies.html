Luscious Lemon Cookies

These luscious Lemon Cookies are like little bites of sunshine! Tender and satisfying, infused with vibrant lemon zest and warming vanilla—an easy one-bowl recipe!

These lemon cookies are sure to brighten your day! The unique addition of semolina flour lends an irresistible texture, while lemon zest and vanilla extract add warmth and vibrancy—the perfect balance of sweet and tart. Once baked, the cookies are dipped in a lemon glaze, enhancing the flavor while maintaining their texture for several days.


Ingredients ⇩

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup semolina (or substitute with fine cornmeal)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, at room temperature
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 large egg, at room temperature
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest

Glaze

  • 1 cup powdered sugar
  • 5 tablespoons fresh lemon juice (less if you prefer a thicker glaze)

Instructions ⇩

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Using a hand mixer or stand mixer (with paddle attachment), cream together the butter and sugar until fluffy, scraping the bowl as needed.
  3. Add the egg and vanilla extract and mix until combined.
  4. Gradually add the flour, semolina, and salt into the wet mixture. Stir until just incorporated to avoid over-mixing.
  5. For a rustic shape: Roll the dough into 2″ x 12″ logs, wrap in parchment, or seal in an airtight container. Chill for 2 hours or overnight. Slice into 1/3″ slices.
  6. For a more polished shape: Roll out the dough until 1/3″ thick and cut with a 2″ round cookie cutter.
  7. Place cookies 1 inch apart on silicone baking mats or parchment-lined baking sheets.
  8. Bake for 12 minutes or until the edges start to brown. Let them cool completely on a wire rack.
  9. To make the glaze: Sift the powdered sugar into a bowl. Whisk in lemon juice until smooth.
  10. Dip the tops of the cookies into the glaze and let the excess drip off by placing the cookies on a rack over parchment paper.
  11. If desired, dip a second time after the first coat has dried for a sweeter result.
  12. Optionally, garnish with candied lemons.
  13. Store the cookies in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 4 days or freeze them in a sealed container for up to 3 months.
