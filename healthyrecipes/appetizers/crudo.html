Crudo

Crudo is easy to make at home and is one of those appetizers that will WOW your guests. Think of crudo like Italian-style sashimi- raw fish drizzled with olive oil, flakey sea salt, citrus, and herbs. We’ll show you the basic recipe to use as a jumping-off point for your own creations. There are many ways to prepare crudo!

For those of you who love sushi, ceviche, and aguachile, this crudo recipe is for you! In our catering business, it was always a hit, disappearing the fastest! When I see it on a restaurant menu (most often as hamachi crudo), it is my favorite thing to order- crudo, especially when prepared well, is so fresh, tasty, and healthy! The best part is how easy it is to make at home, with truly the simplest of ingredients!


Ingredients ⇩

  • 8 ounces of wild ocean fish (skin off): halibut, escolar, sea bass, hamachi, tuna (yellow tail, ahi), rockfish, red snapper, ono, or scallops (freeze for 30 minutes)

Basic Crudo Dressing

  • 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon fresh black pepper

Optional Additions

  • Spicy: chili flakes (Aleppo or urfa biber), thinly sliced fresh chilies (serrano, red, jalapeño), or Calabrian chilies
  • Fresh herbs: basil, cilantro, dill, Italian parsley, tarragon, mint, microgreens
  • Crunchy: cucumber, radish, pickled onions, watermelon radish
  • Capers
  • Toasted pine nuts
  • Arugula
  • Gremolata
  • Flaky sea salt

Instructions ⇩

  1. Freeze fish for 20-30 minutes to make it easier to slice.
  2. Make the Crudo Dressing. Zest the lemon and set the zest aside. Mix the olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper in a bowl. Set this aside.
  3. Slice the fish into very thin slices.
  4. Spoon half of the whisked crudo dressing on a plate, covering the area where you plan to put the fish. Arrange the fish in a row, circle, scattered, or however you like.
  5. Tuck in veggies, herbs, chilies, and whatever additions you want to use, then spoon the remaining crudo dressing over the top of the fish.
  6. Finish with the zest and chili flakes.
  7. Refrigerate until serving. Best served cold!
  8. Leftovers are good for one day.
